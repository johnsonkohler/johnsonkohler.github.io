Some recent research and opinions on how LLMs are affecting our world.

I should probably put it on record at the outset that I think LLMs are a net negative.

AI companies being evil

In case you want to get away from the corporate oligopoly but don't know how:

  • The web browser Firefox is really lightweight and fast nowadays. It's also not full of spyware.
  • While you're at it, you could set your default web browser to DuckDuckGo or Startpage!
  • LibreOffice is like Microsoft Office, but free, open-source, and (as with Firefox) not filled with spyware. Note: unlike its Microsoft and Google counterparts, there's no web-hosted version.
  • If you absolutely NEED an online text editor (few people do), there aren't as many great feature-rich options. Here's what I have:
    • For very lightweight, non-collaborative note-taking, try Writebox. It's a blank box you can put words in. It also supports Markdown.
    • If you want a similarly lightweight editor but with the collaboration-ness of Google Docs, and you're interested in self-hosting (easier than it sounds!), try Hedgedoc.
    • If, on the other hand, you want to format a document EXACTLY a certain way, and you want Docs-like collaboration, try Overleaf. You'll have to learn LaTeX, not Markdown, but the things you'll be able to do will make Docs look like a typewriter.
  • And, finally, if you hate the ads and algorithms and echo chambers of Meta/TikTok/etc., try Mastodon. It's not a single website, which is part of what makes it better! It's a network of sites ("instances") that have separate admins but talk to each other as if they were one. Your feed is the people you're subscribed to (at the moment Mastodon is mostly queer people, nerds, leftists, and artists) and the people they boost, in chronological order. That's it. And if you don't like how your instance is run, you can just switch to a different one.
